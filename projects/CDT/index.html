Summary

Background

The Center for Digital Transformation (CDT) is a Center of Excellence at The Paul Merage School of Business at the University of California, Irvine. The Center conducts and disseminates research on digital transformation. As a university-based center, it focuses on educating the workforce, advancing the competitiveness and productivity of business in the digital economy.

Roles I Played

  • UX Designer
  • Front-end Developer

Goals

  1. Host signature conference information
    • Road to Reinvention is an annual conference that provides thought-provoking conversations with leaders and luminaries and bring together experienced executives, technology leaders, and the latest academic thinking with one goal: to help others navigate the road ahead.
  2. Grow subscriber base
  3. Allows hosting and gating of research reports

Challenges

  1. While Divi and Elementor are extremely similar, there were certain nuances that were difficult to overcome.
  2. The original CDT website had 5 years worth of conference data that was either not being shown or severely outdated in appearance. The site lacked consistency in its conference pages as the years progressed which made the site look unpolished.
  3. A standard and consistency needed to be created in order for future employees to be able to achieve the same look with new pages.

Solutions

  1. Limit access to most recent research reports in order to grow subscriber base but minimizing the amount of clicks needed to sign up.
  2. Use a website builder that allowed more freedom in customization and better support for iterative development.

Competitive Analysis

The main competitors of CDT are centers of research and excellence that focus on similar missions and target similar audiences such as:

  • MIT Initiative on the Digital Economy
  • MIT Center for Information Systems

MIT Initiative on the Digital Economy (IDE) is effective in clearly stating their mission statement directly on their landing page. They also include a huge Call to Action that allows users to learn more. The News, Events, and Media section below the header brings attention to the most current events happening within IDE.
