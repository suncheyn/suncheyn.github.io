Problem
The task for this project was to completely redesign CDT's website in order to better fit their functional needs as well as increase web traffic. The main focal points were to make the website easier for clients to navigate and shorten interaction times to complete specific actions.

Solution
Use a website builder that allows more freedom in customization and better support for future iterative development.

Role
I played two key roles in this project as both UX Designer and Front-End Developer. I created the prototypes for the website redesign as well as implemented the design using Divi, a website editor, along with custom HTML and CSS elements.

Summary

Background

The Center for Digital Transformation (CDT) is a Center of Excellence at The Paul Merage School of Business at the University of California, Irvine. The Center conducts and disseminates research on digital transformation. As a university-based center, it focuses on educating the workforce, advancing the competitiveness and productivity of business in the digital economy.

Objectives

This website serves a multitude of purposes for CDT but there are three main areas we focused on in order to improve the functionality of the site.

Showcase Signature Conference

Road to Reinvention is CDT's biggest event and their website is used as the primary place to access information months leading up to the conference.
Compile Previous Content

CDT has years worth of information from their previous conferences, events, research and articles that they would like to preserve on the new website.
Gate Research Reports

CDT have research reports that they publish annually and would like to keep the most recent ones behind an email subscription in order to grow their ccontact list.

Challenges

  1. While Divi and Elementor are extremely similar, there were certain nuances for each of the platforms that were difficult to overcome.
  2. The original CDT website had 5 years worth of conference data that was either not being shown or severely outdated in appearance. The site lacked consistency in its pages as the years progressed which made the site look unpolished.
  3. A standard and consistency needed to be created in order for future developnent to be streamlined.

Solutions

  1. Limit access to most recent research reports in order to grow subscriber base but minimizing the amount of clicks needed to sign up.
  2. Use a website builder that allowed more freedom in customization and better support for iterative development.
  3. Integrate Google Analytics to be able to track website traffic.

Design Process

Competitive Analysis

The main competitors of CDT are centers of research and excellence that focus on similar missions and target similar audiences such as:

MIT Initiative on the Digital Economy

Strengths

  • Clearly emphasizes their mission statement directly on their landing page
  • Call to Action front and center that allows users to learn more
  • The News, Events, and Media section below the header brings attention to the most current events happening

Weaknesses

  • Information overload
Center for the Digital Future

Strengths

  • Displays reports immediately on front page
  • Showcases their different social media accounts prominently

Weaknesses

  • Extremely word heavy with few visuals
Techonomy

Strengths

  • Highlights videos from their annual conference

Weaknesses

  • Navigation is lengthy
