Problem
There is a huge market for personal safety and emergency situations in which people would need help.

Solution
I wanted to fill this gap with a product that would allow people to feel safe when they're alone and reassure them that if something did happen, they would be okay.

Summary

Background

Assure is a mobile application and wearable that addresses concerns of sexual harassment. If you’ve ever felt wary walking out at night by yourself, unsure of how to protect yourself from a harasser, or want a space to communicate with others who’ve gone through similar situations, Assure aims to provide solutions to address these very problems. Currently, there is a lack of resources as well as support available for individuals who may be at risk or who have experienced some form of sexual harassment. This product aims to fill that gap by providing a set of features that help a user to prevent, protect, and recover from incidents of sexual harassment.

Objectives

Prevent

Finding a way for users to protect themselves in dangerous situations.
Protect

Create a space for users to communicate with others when in an emergency.
Recover

Support individuals who may be at risk or who have experienced some form of sexual harassment.

Challenges

We found that there were gaps within our initial prototype and overall design. We uncovered pain points as well as unintuitive flows through prototypes and user testing. Some gaps included the lack of a panic button on the mobile device as well as a space for the user’s personal information. Four our MVP design, we addressed the latter by adding in the Profiles tab a section that housed information surrounding the user’s forum engagements (comments, liked posts, responses) as well as their incidents and reports. We also added in a panic button on mobile that could be pressed anytime during the user’s trip. Following our first iteration and tests, we updated the reporting flow to better distinguish between creating reports from scratch and from a recorded incident.

Solutions

Prevent

Prevent attacks from occuring in the first place using an interactive map that allows users to avoid potential unsafe areas using reports gathered from other Assure users.
Protect

Protect users by keeping others Keep others informed of where the user's location, and alerting them if things go south.
Recover

Recover and heal. The community forum is a place for individuals looking for a supportive environment to heal in or for individuals to find more information about ways to protect themselves or get justice and overall, just a place to connect with others and basically tackle the issue of sexual harassment together. Users can create posts, look and read through other posts and pages, as well as interact with other posts and users by commenting and liking posts and messages.

Design Process

User Interviews

A user interview is a UX research method where a designer/researcher learns about the users understanding of the topic, goals, challenges, behaviors and/or habits. It’s a way for designers to tap into the user’s feet and be able to empathize with them.

Research Implementation

We started by defining our goals for the interview. These included:

  • To understand people's conceptualization of harassment
  • To find out what measures people currently take when thgey feel unsafe
  • To see waht actions they would want to take under such circumstances

We created a list of questions we wanted to ask the interviewees that would help us address our goals. For example, we asked:

  • Have you ever felt afraid of going out on your own? When? Why?
  • What protective measures do your normally take?

Findings

We used affinity diagramming in order to cateogorize and identify trends among the responses. The key takeaways were:

Presence of other people helped thwart potential advances
People avoided walking at night and in dangerous areas
People found that reporting incidents was unnecessary. They instead spoke with friends or family to cope.
During uncomfortable situations, people did not have enough time to react and respond

Surveys

Surveys consist of specific sets of questions and are requested to be filled out by a representative set of users. It is used by UX researchers/designers to understand the problem space better and gain quantitative information that can be used to draw insights along with qualitative data from interviews.

Research Implementation

We started by defining goals and creating a list of questions for the survey. The questions on the survey were broken down into 3 sections - General Info, Personal Experience and Witnessing. Questions included:

  • How safe do you feel to walk alone during the day/night?
  • If you have experienced sexual harassment, where did it take place? (optional — feel free to omit this if you are not comfortable)

Findings

Some the key insights from the survey include:

61% of respondents felt unsafe walking at night.
66% of respondents have experienced some form of harassment in the past.
48% of respondents were not so likely to report an incident of harassment.
Walking with someone is one of the most effective protective measures.
